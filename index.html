Background

Scope of U.S. Rental Subsidy and Study

This platform is an interactive research tool examining the federal rental assistance system in the United States. Three major programs — Housing Choice Vouchers, Section 8 Project-Based Rental Assistance, and Public Housing — assist about 84 percent of the households receiving federal rental assistance. The platform integrates national rental assistance data with local socioeconomic indicators to provide a spatial understanding of subsidy distribution and voucher reliance across all 50 states.

Much of the subsidized housing stock was developed before 1980 and operates through expiring contracts rather than permanent affordability. As contracts terminate and rental markets become increasingly unaffordable, Housing Choice Vouchers (HCV) have become the primary federal safety net. These shifts create uneven spatial risks that require integrated, accessible analysis.

Website Objective

  1. Aggregate national rental subsidy datasets with local contextual indicators into a unified platform.
  2. Visualize subsidy distribution and rental market conditions at multiple geographic scales.
  3. Identify spatial mismatches between subsidy loss, housing need, and market rent pressures.
  4. Support focused research modules and policy scenario analysis for equity-oriented decision making.

PHA Level Performance Metrics

Indicator Definitions

Indicator Definition Calculation
Reserve Balance Funding reserves are unspent voucher assistance funds that an agency received in prior years. Yearly mean of (HCV Unrestricted Net Position as of last day of month + HCV Restricted Net Position Funds as of last day of month)
Success Rate The percent of vouchers an agency has issued to families in a year that result in an actual lease and assistance contract with a landlord. Yearly mean of (Vouchers Under Lease / (Vouchers Under Lease + New Vouchers Issued but not under HAP contract))
Utilization Rate Measures what share of a housing authority's voucher budget is currently leased up in vouchers. Yearly mean of (Vouchers Under Lease / Total Vouchers)
Cost Per Voucher Average monthly HAP expenditure per leased voucher, covering all voucher program types including Litigation, Mainstream, Homeownership, MTW, Family Unification, NED, Portable, HOPE VI, Tenant Protection, VASH, DHAP-HCV, and FSS Escrow. Yearly mean of (Sum of all monthly HAP expenses / Vouchers Under Lease)
Port-In Rate Share of leased vouchers that are portable vouchers administered by the PHA on behalf of another agency. Yearly mean of (Portable Vouchers Administered (Port-In) / Vouchers Under Lease)

Datasets & References

Primary Data Sources

  • HUD Voucher Management System (VMS) — PHA-level monthly voucher utilization, leasing, and financial data
  • HUD Picture of Subsidized Households — National, state, county, census tract, and PHA-level subsidized housing counts
  • HUD Fair Market Rents (FMR) / Small Area FMR (SAFMR) — Annual subsidy benchmarks
  • American Community Survey (ACS) — Renter population, poverty, and demographic indicators
  • Zillow ZORI — Market rent indicators (optional overlay)

Core Variable Definitions

  • Utilization Rate: Vouchers Under Lease / Total Vouchers
  • Success Rate: Vouchers Leased / (Vouchers Leased + Issued but not leased)
  • Reserve Balance: Unrestricted Net Position + Restricted Net Position Funds
  • Cost Per Voucher: Total HAP Expenses / Vouchers Under Lease
  • Port-In Rate: Portable Vouchers Administered / Vouchers Under Lease
  • Voucher Gap Ratio: Actual Vouchers / Potential Vouchers × Renter Population weight

Methodology Notes

All metrics are calculated as yearly means of monthly VMS values. Geographic units vary by module: PHA boundaries for performance metrics; ZIP code and metro for gap and opportunity analyses. Data coverage spans available HUD reporting years.
